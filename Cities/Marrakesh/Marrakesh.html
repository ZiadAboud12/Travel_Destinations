Overview

Marrakesh, the regional capital, constitutes a prefecture-level administrative unit of Morocco, Marrakech Prefecture, forming part of the region of Marrakech-Safi. Marrakesh is a major centre for law and jurisdiction in Morocco, and most of the major courts of the region are here. it is known for its rich history, colourful markets, and unique blend of Arab, Berber, and French influences. Famous sites include the bustling Jemaa el-Fnaa square, the historic Koutoubia Mosque, and the beautiful Majorelle Garden.

The Koutoubia Mosque

The Koutoubia Mosque, located in Marrakech, Morocco, is the city’s largest and most iconic mosque, dating back to the 12th century. Known for its striking 77-meter minaret, visible across Marrakech, the mosque is an architectural masterpiece of the Almohad dynasty. It remains an active place of worship and a symbol of Marrakech’s rich Islamic heritage.

Overview

Istanbul, Turkey’s largest city, is renowned for its rich history, stunning architecture, and unique location bridging Europe and Asia. Known for landmarks like the Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, and Topkapi Palace, Istanbul reflects a blend of Byzantine, Ottoman, and modern influences. Its vibrant bazaars, such as the Grand Bazaar and scenic Bosphorus views, make it a cultural and historic hub. It is considered the country's economic, cultural, and historic capital. The city has a population of over 15 million residents, comprising 19% of the population of Turkey, and is the most popular city.
